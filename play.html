SAM AVERAGE by PERCY MACKAYE


_Sam Average_ is reprinted by special permission of Percy Mackaye. This play first appeared in _Yankee Fantasies_, Duffield & Company, New York.

JOEL.

[_With a singing call._]

Four o'clock!--All's well!

[_Jumping down from the embankment]

he approaches the fire._

ANDREW.

By God, Joel, it's bitter.

JOEL.

[_Rubbing his hands over the coals._]

A mite sharpish.

ANDREW.

[_Looks up eagerly._]

What?

JOEL.

Cuts sharp, for Thanksgivin'

ANDREW.

[_Sinks back, gloomily._]

Oh!

[_A pause._]

I wondered you should agree with me. You meant the weather. I meant--

[_A pause again._]

JOEL.

Well, Andy, what'd you mean?

ANDREW.

Life.
